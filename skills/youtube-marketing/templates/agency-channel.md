# Channel Template — Agency Channel

## When to Use
Use this template when:
- Channel owner sells web design, development, or digital marketing services
- Goal = qualified client leads, not product sales or ad revenue
- Audience = business owners, marketers, non-technical decision makers

## Channel Constants

```
Agency name: [Name]
Services: [Web design / Dev / SEO / etc.]
Contact/inquiry: [URL or email]
Portfolio: [URL]
Case studies: [URL]
Subscribe URL: https://www.youtube.com/c/[handle]/?sub_confirmation=1
```

## Content Strategy

Agency channels work differently — the audience is CLIENTS, not other developers:

| Content Type | % | Why |
|-------------|---|-----|
| "Results we got for [client type]" | 30% | Proof of work = trust |
| "How to [business outcome] with [tool]" | 35% | Searches clients actually run |
| Process transparency ("How we build...") | 20% | Positions as expert, filters serious leads |
| Industry-specific tutorials | 15% | Niche authority ("dentist website", "law firm website") |

**Do NOT**: Make highly technical coding tutorials — that attracts other developers, not clients.
**DO**: Make outcome-focused tutorials — "How to get more leads from your website."

## CTA Strategy

- Primary CTA: "Book a free call" or "See our portfolio"
- Never: "Buy now" (service business = relationship first)
- Build trust through the video, ask for the call at the end
- Description should have a clear contact mechanism

## Keyword Strategy

Agency channels should target what their CLIENTS search:
- "How to [improve business thing] with WordPress"
- "Best [niche] website design [year]"
- "[City] web design" (if local)
- "[Industry] website examples" (e.g., "restaurant website design")

Not: "How to code X in WordPress" — that's for developers, not clients.

## Thumbnail Style

- Show polished website designs — aspirational
- Business-language titles: "More Leads from Your Website" > "CSS Grid Tutorial"
- Clean, professional aesthetic matching your agency brand
- Avoid overly technical-looking thumbnails
