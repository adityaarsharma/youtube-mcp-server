# Retention Scripting Guide

## The Hook Window (0:00–0:42)

The first 42 seconds are the highest drop-off window. Structure them as 3 beats:

```
GRAB (0:00–0:10)   — Show the result or state the surprising thing
PROMISE (0:10–0:28) — What they'll be able to do after this video
STAKES (0:28–0:42) — Why this matters / what problem it solves
```

**Optimal hook length**: 28–42 seconds (Retention Rabbit, 150M+ minutes analysis)
- Under 28s: Not enough time to establish why they should stay
- Over 42s: People leave before getting to the content

**Never start with**: "Hey guys, welcome back to the channel, don't forget to like and subscribe..."
This kills 15–25% of viewers before you start.

## Pattern Interrupts (Every 60–90s)

After the hook, viewers drop off progressively. Pattern interrupts reset attention:

| Type | How to Use |
|------|-----------|
| Visual cut | Switch from talking head to screen recording |
| Text overlay | Pop a key stat or result on screen |
| B-roll | Show the finished result mid-tutorial |
| Pace change | Speed up a repetitive step with text overlay |
| Question | "Now here's where most people get stuck..." |

Wistia (2024): Pattern interrupts every 60–90s reduce drop-off by 40%.

## The 25% Mark CTA

Place a soft subscribe/product CTA at 25% through the video — not the end.
- Most viewers who leave early do so by 50% — waiting until the end loses them
- "If this is useful, grab the free plugin below — link in description" is enough
- Don't hard sell — viewers drop if they feel sold to mid-tutorial

## The Tutorial Body

For step-by-step tutorials, every step needs:
1. **What**: Name the step
2. **Why**: One sentence on why this setting matters
3. **How**: Screen recording with audio — the actual action
4. **Confirm**: Show the result before moving to next step

Skip "Why" for obvious steps — only include it when the viewer would otherwise wonder.

## Retention Risk Points

| Timestamp | Risk | Fix |
|-----------|------|-----|
| 0:00–0:10 | No hook | Show result in first 3 seconds |
| 0:42–1:30 | Slow intro | Cut to content by 45s |
| Mid-video (setup steps) | Repetitive | Speed up + text overlay |
| Near end (outro) | CTA overload | One CTA max, 15 seconds |

## Energy Patterns (AIR Media-Tech)

5 energy states to vary throughout a tutorial:
1. **Demonstration** — show the tool in action
2. **Explanation** — narrate what's happening
3. **Problem/Solution** — state the issue, reveal the fix
4. **Result reveal** — show finished output
5. **Context** — why this matters for their use case

Rotate these to prevent monotony. Never 3 consecutive steps of pure explanation.
